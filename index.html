About me

I am a postdoc in the Department of Mechanical Science and Engineering at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, advised by Prof. Naira Hovakimyan. From 2013 to 2018, I was a PhD student in the Control Engineering Lab at the University of British Columbia, where I was supervised by Prof. Ryozo Nagamune and Prof. Mu Chiao. I have also been very fortunate to work with Prof. Shaoping Wang during my master's studies at Beihang University from 2008 to 2013. Before starting my Ph.D. study, I was a modeling & simulation engineer in Hirain Technologies.

Research Interests

My research interests include control, autonomy and sustainability. In particular, I am interested in developing control and learning algorithms to ensure safe and efficient operation of robotic and autonomous systems in unknown and unpreditable environments. I am also fascinated by the potentials of control and machine learning techniques in helping achieve sustainable agriculture and mitigate climate change.
